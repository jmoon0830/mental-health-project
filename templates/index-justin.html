Inspiration

Mental health in the workplace is a serious and important topic. According to a Harvard study, almost 20% of American employees from ages 15-54 experience symptoms of mental health disorders. In addition, these employees may not even seek help because they feel it could jeaopardize their jobs. As there is a connection between mental health and productivity in the workplace, this website pulls datasets from 2014 and 2016 to find signficances in employees' responses to questions regarding mental health in the workplace.

Where Were People Surveyed?

Comfortabilty Factors

There were three questions that revolved around how comfortable employees felt.

  1. Coworkers - Would you be willing to discuss a mental health issue with your coworkers?
  2. Supervisors - Would you be willing to discuss a mental health issue with your direct supervisor(s)?
  3. Anonymity - Is your anonymity protected if you choose to take advantage of mental health or substance abuse treatment resources?
